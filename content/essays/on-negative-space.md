The composer speaks of rests as though they were notes, because they are. The rest is not the absence of music; it is the part of the music that lets the rest of it mean something. Remove the silences and you do not get more music. You get noise.

I have come to believe the same is true of nearly everything worth making — and of the day itself.

## The unfilled hour

We have grown suspicious of empty time, as though it were a defect to be optimised away. But the empty hour is where thinking happens. It is the negative space around the work, and it is doing exactly as much for the work as the rest does for the phrase.

A page with generous margins is not wasting paper. It is telling you how to read.

## Confidence, again

It takes a certain confidence to leave space — to trust that less will register as more, that the reader will lean in rather than drift away. The insecure design fills every corner. The confident one lets the corner be a corner.

There is a version of luxury that is really just abundance: more, better, rarer. And there is another version, quieter and harder to buy, that is about proportion — knowing exactly how much is enough and then stopping.

I am interested in the second kind.
