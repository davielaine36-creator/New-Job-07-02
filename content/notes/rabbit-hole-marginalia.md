I trust a person's margins more than their bookshelf.

The shelf is a performance — curated, dusted, arranged for the room. The margins are private. A pencil line, a small *yes* beside a paragraph, a question mark that turns into an argument three pages later. That's where the reading actually happened.

A clean book has not been read. It has been stored.
